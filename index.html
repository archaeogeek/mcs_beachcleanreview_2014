Plastics in the Sea

80%

of marine debris is derived from plastics

10-100 items per km2 in English Channel

1 million items per km2 in North Pacific Gyre

How does it get there?

  • Dumping at sea (although illegal since 1988)
  • Marine fishing debris
  • Rivers
  • Sewage
  • Beach litter

Why is it a problem?

It gets eaten by sea creatures

At least 267 marine species are known to have suffered from plastic ingestion or entanglement

Microplastics are bad for crustaceans and worms

They enter through gills and take 6 times longer to leave than through standard digestion

Harmful chemicals such as flame retardants are ingested

They may also retard digestion and reduce energy levels

Can produce a severe immunological response

It doesn't biodegrade

It disintegrates into smaller pieces, whilst remaining a polymer

Decomposition may concentrate pollutants and leach out toxins such as PCBs

Affect on human health

  • Direct toxicity from lead, cadmium, mercury
  • Carcinogens, birth defects, immune problems, child development defects from di-ethylhexyl phthalate (DEHP)
  • Human hormonal disruption from Bisphenol-A (BPA)
